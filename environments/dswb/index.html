Overview

Data Scientist Workbench aims to be your one-stop shop for data science tools. At this time, it is a technology preview limited to IPython/Jupyter notebooks. We recommend that you use notebooks as the way to visualize, document and present your analysis. Currently, notebooks can be written in either Python or R.

Requesting your own Data Scientist Workbench

Go to Data Scientist Workbench and click the big blue button.

Sharing Notebooks

Sharing notebooks can be very useful not only for team collaboration, but as a means to present your results publicly.

Sharing a notebook

  1. Go to Data Scientist Workbench.
  2. On the menu, click "My Notebooks".
  3. Find the notebook you want to share, and click the twistie to the right of it.
  4. Click the share button
  5. Click the "Show Link" link to get the link to the shared notebook.

Importing Notebooks

  1. Copy the link of the notebook you want to import (from step 5 ).
  2. Go to Data Scientist Workbench and click "My Notbooks".
  3. Paste the link in the text field at the top right corner of the page.
  4. Hit "Enter" to import the notebook.

Visualize your Analysis Results

  1. User Spark to write results to CSV files.
  2. Transfer from HDFS to local filesystem.
  3. Download the files to your laptop using the Uploads/Downloads service.
  4. Go to Data Scientist Notebooks and drag/drop your CSV files in it.
